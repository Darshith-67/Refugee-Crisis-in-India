Borderless Lives: Refugee Crisis in India

This site presents a data-driven academic overview of the refugee situation in India, based on UNHCR, NHRC, and other official sources.

The Refugee Crisis

India is home to multiple refugee groups from neighboring countries, but it does not have a national refugee law. Refugees are accommodated under the Foreigners Act on a case-by-case basis.

By the end of 2024, approximately 236,768 refugees and asylum-seekers were registered in India.

Main causes of displacement:

  • Armed conflicts in Afghanistan, Myanmar, and Sri Lanka
  • Political persecution and ethnic discrimination
  • Religious persecution and statelessness

Refugee Communities

Tibetan Refugees

Approx. 72,291 registered. Primarily settled in Dharamshala, Karnataka, and Uttarakhand. Community emphasizes education and cultural preservation.

Sri Lankan Tamils

Approx. 92,015 registered. Concentrated in Tamil Nadu. Many arrived during the civil war (1983-2009).

Rohingya / Myanmar Refugees

Approx. 30,142 registered. Mostly living in Jammu, Hyderabad, and Delhi. Vulnerable to statelessness and lack of access to legal work.

Afghan Refugees

Approx. 14,578 registered. Many arrived after Soviet occupation in the 1980s and during Taliban resurgence.

Other Groups

Chakma, Hajong, and other smaller groups reside in northeast India and urban centers.

Geographical Distribution

  • Delhi: Afghan refugees, Rohingya
  • Jammu: Rohingya
  • Tamil Nadu: Sri Lankan Tamils
  • Dharamshala: Tibetan Government-in-Exile
  • Northeast India: Chakma, Hajong
